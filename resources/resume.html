Work History

  • Podest Podcast App

    2015 - 2018 Developer/Founder, Walsrode

    I wrote Podest, a modest podcast client for iOS, focusing on performance and efficiency. Caching data remotely and locally makes searching and browsing a snappy and unintrusive experience. Podest’s minimalist user interface sets the stage for awesome podcasts. Building an app of this complexity alone and shipping it has been challenging. Picking up Swift along the way, a language in the making, sounds harder than it was. The cognitive interplay between writing JavaScript—I use Node for centralized feed parsing and caching—and Swift proved itself as surprisingly fruitful.

  • 2008 - 2011 Developer, CMC Markets, London

    Starting with a distributed team of five, for CMC, we brought their spread betting platform to the web. With the team growing to around 50 people, we built a rich, modular Adobe Flash application. I was responsible for the product library and the trading ticket of the Flash client. This integral role allowed me to catalyse and harmonize team communication between groups.

  • 2002 - 2008 Consulting, DRID, Bassier, Bergmann & Kindler; Kuborgh, and more

    In the heyday of Adobe Flash, we developed product sites, games, and banners, selling everything between packet soup to luxory cars. Memorable is the 2006 FIFA World Cup in Germany, for which I built a stylable betting game with DRID, which was sold to multiple clients.

  • 2000 - 2001 Screen Designer, Pixelpark, Hamburg
  • 1996 - 1999 Graphic Designer/Founder, i&i visual communication, Hamburg
